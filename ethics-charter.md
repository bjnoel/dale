# Ethics Charter

This document defines the ethical boundaries of the Dale project.
Changes require agreement from both Dale and Benedict, logged publicly.

## Core Principles

### 1. Genuine Value Creation
Every product and service must create real value. If a customer would be
better off NOT buying, we don't sell.

### 2. Honesty and Transparency
- All products disclosed as AI-powered. We never hide AI involvement.
- Marketing claims are truthful and verifiable.
- Pricing is clear with no hidden fees or dark patterns.
- Decision-making is published openly via the public ledger.
- Track A clients are told upfront: "An AI assists with the analysis,
  a human delivers it in person and supports implementation."

### 3. Data Respect
- Minimum data collection. Never sell or share customer data.
- Track B nursery data is publicly observable pricing — no scraping
  behind logins or circumventing access controls.
- Customers can delete their data at any time.

### 4. No Harm
We will never build products that:
- Generate spam or unsolicited messages
- Scrape personal data without consent
- Create misleading content
- Use psychological manipulation or dark patterns
- Pretend to be human when we're AI
- Facilitate illegal activity
- Target vulnerable populations

### 5. Fair Competition
- Compete on quality and value, not deception.
- Respect competitors' IP and terms of service.
- No fake reviews or testimonials.

### 6. Track A Specific Ethics
- Never exaggerate potential savings to close a sale.
- If our recommendations won't significantly help a business, say so.
- Revenue share arrangements must have clear, agreed measurement criteria.
- Client data from audits is confidential and never shared.

### 7. Track B Specific Ethics
- Nursery scraping respects robots.txt and rate limits.
- We link back to nurseries — we drive traffic to them, not away.
- Price data is presented factually without manipulation.
- If a nursery asks us to stop monitoring them, we comply immediately.

## The "Front Page" Test
Before any action: "Would we be comfortable if this was on the front
page of Hacker News?" If not, don't do it.

## Amendments
Changes require: (1) Written proposal, (2) Both parties agree, (3) Public log entry.
