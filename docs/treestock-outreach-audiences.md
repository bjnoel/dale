# Treestock outreach: Australian groups and organisations

Research date: 2026-06-12 (Benedict requested). Web research into groups whose
members would plausibly use treestock.com.au (rare fruit nursery stock
aggregation, price tracking, restock alerts).

Outreach rules that apply to everything below:
- Two-touch approach (feedback_outreach_approach): relationship and usefulness
  first, never a cold pitch. Treestock is a free community tool, lead with that.
- Customer-facing messaging is drafted by Dale, reviewed by Benedict.
- WAAS is beekeepers. Beestock audience, never treestock (feedback_waas_is_beekeepers).
- Daleys forum is off limits: Daleys is a nursery we scrape. Promoting a price
  comparison tool on their own forum is bad form and risks the data source.
  The same caution applies to any nursery-run community.

## Tier 1: Rare fruit clubs (perfect fit, the core audience)

These are treestock's exact demographic: collectors who hunt specific varieties
and complain about stock availability.

| Group | Where | Channels | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rare Fruit Club WA | Perth/SW WA | rarefruitclub.au, meetings | Benedict's home club. Warm intro already in motion (DAL-171 drafts). Best first move. |
| Rare Fruit Society of South Australia | Adelaide | rarefruit-sa.org.au, digital newsletter, Facebook page | Active, paid membership ($35/$40), runs field trips and workshops. Newsletter mention is the natural slot. |
| Sub-Tropical Fruit Club of Qld | Brisbane | stfc.org.au, Facebook (@SubTropicalFruitsQLD), bi-monthly meetings | Explicitly covers rare fruit AND bush food (relevant to the bush tucker pilot). Site has a fruit club links directory worth mining for more contacts. |
| Rare Fruit Australia Inc (FNQ and other QLD branches) | Cairns and regional QLD | rarefruitaustralia.org, FNQ branch Facebook | Descendant of the Rare Fruit Council of Australia. Bi-monthly field days. Tropical stock focus matches Daleys/Ross Creek coverage. |
| Fraser Coast Sub-Tropical Fruit Club | Hervey Bay region QLD | via stfc.org.au links page | Smaller sister club, same profile. |
| Heritage and Rare Fruit Network (Australia) | National, Tasmania-leaning | Facebook group + heritageandrarefruits.weebly.com | Standout find: their "Sources of Fruit Trees" page is a hand-maintained directory of heritage nurseries by state, i.e. a manual version of what treestock automates. They already link Rare Fruit Club WA. Natural ally, approach via the FB group. |

Note: RFCA archives (rfcarchives.org.au) are the Benedict-owned archive asset
(DEC-127), already a treestock citation source. The living RFCA branches above
are distinct from the archive.

## Tier 2: Heritage orchard and grafting groups (strong fit)

Variety hunters by definition. Scion and grafting people care exactly about
"who stocks this cultivar".

| Group | Where | Channels | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Heritage Fruits Society | Petty's Orchard, Templestowe VIC | heritagefruitssociety.org.au | ~280 heritage apple varieties, sells trees and graft wood, runs tastings. Members research varieties, ideal users of variety pages and watches. |
| Werribee Park Heritage Orchard | Werribee VIC | werribeeparkheritageorchard.org.au | Volunteer group, weekly sessions, two public grafting days a year (Feb and July). Grafting day attendees are prime Treesmith users too (graft tracking). |
| Grove heritage collection / Tahune Fields | Huon TAS | via Heritage and Rare Fruit Network | ~600 cultivar collection. Reach via the Tier 1 network rather than directly. |

## Tier 3: Permaculture network (good fit, big reach)

Food forest people buy a lot of fruit trees. State bodies have newsletters and
local group directories.

| Group | Where | Channels | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| PermacultureWest | WA | permaculturewest.org.au, local group list | Local advantage, Benedict can show up in person. |
| Permaculture Australia | National | permacultureaustralia.org.au directory | Peak body. Directory listing for treestock may be possible. |

| Pip Magazine | National | pipmagazine.com.au, print + digital + podcast | Permaculture media. A "free tool for finding rare fruit trees" mention or article pitch. |

## Tier 4: Community gardening and organic growers (moderate fit)

Broader gardeners, a slice of whom are fruit tree buyers.

| Group | Where | Channels | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Community Gardens Australia | National | communitygarden.org.au, Facebook | Peak body with state contacts. Low effort: one email to the national newsletter. |
| Canberra Organic Growers Society | ACT | cogs.asn.au, quarterly magazine, 500+ members | Active, has a links page that already lists growing resources. |
| Sustainable Gardening Australia | National | sgaonline.org.au | Resource publisher, runs fruit tree articles. A links/resources listing. |
| Local Food Connect | Eltham VIC | localfoodconnect.org.au | Runs a local food directory including fruit trees on public land. Directory-minded people. |
| CERES Urban Orchard swap network | Melbourne + interstate copies | via CERES | Produce swappers, weaker fit but large. |
| Seed Savers groups | National | seedsavers.net, seedsavers.org.au | Seeds not trees, weakest fit in this tier. Park unless free. |
