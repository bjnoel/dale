# The Approach Script

How Benedict introduces the service to potential clients. Keep it natural —
this is a conversation guide, not a script to read verbatim.

## The Cold Approach (walking into a business)

**Opening (10 seconds):**
"Hey, I'm Ben — I run a small consultancy called Walkthrough. I help
Perth businesses find practical ways to save time and money with technology.
Not the fancy stuff — just things you can actually do this week."

**The hook (20 seconds):**
"I do a one-hour walkthrough of your business — watch how things work, have
a chat about what's frustrating — and come back with 3-5 specific things
you can change. No jargon, no 40-page report. Just practical stuff."

**If they're interested:**
"It's $199 for the full assessment. That includes the visit, the analysis,
and a 30-minute session where I walk you through everything and answer
questions. Most clients find at least one thing that pays for the assessment
in the first week."

**If they ask about AI:**
"I use AI to analyse your online presence, compare you to similar businesses,
and crunch the data. But the visit is me — I'm the one watching how you
work and talking to you about what matters. The AI is a tool, not a replacement."

**If they're hesitant:**
"Tell you what — I'm building up portfolio pieces at the moment. I could do
yours at a reduced rate / free, and all I'd ask is that if you find it useful,
you'd be happy for me to mention you as a case study. No pressure."

**Closing:**
"Here's my card. Have a think about it. The website's walkthrough.au if you
want to see what a report looks like. No hard sell — just give me a call if
you're interested."

## The Friends / Portfolio Approach

For the first three prospects (John & Felicity at Wembley Cycles / Gather
Ceramics, Paul at PBR Plumbing) — these are friends, not cold prospects.
Don't pitch. Just be honest:

"Hey [Name], I'm starting something new — a consultancy called Walkthrough
where I look at how businesses run and find practical ways to save time with
technology. I'm building up my portfolio and I'd love to use yours as one of
my first case studies. Completely free — I just need an hour to watch how
things work, and I'll come back with 3-5 specific things you could change.
All I'd ask in return is honest feedback on whether it was useful, and if
it is, that you're happy for me to reference you when I talk to other
businesses. No pressure either way."

**If they ask what's in it for you:**
"Honestly? I need real examples to show other businesses. A report based on
a real Perth shop is worth more than any marketing I could write. And I
genuinely think I'll find stuff that's useful for you — I've already had
a look at your website and spotted a few things."

**If they want specifics (use the prospect briefs):**
- John (Wembley Cycles): "I noticed you're still phone-only for service
  bookings — there's a tool called Velodrop that plugs into Lightspeed..."
- Paul (PBR): "Your strata clients like JLL — how do they track job history
  and compliance docs? Because there's a really clean opportunity there..."
- Felicity (Gather): "Did you know you don't have a Google Business Profile?
  That's a 10-minute fix that would get you showing up in 'pottery classes
  near me' searches..."

**After the free assessment — the retainer pitch:**
"If you found this useful and want ongoing help actually implementing the
changes, I offer a monthly retainer for $149. But no pressure — the
assessment is the assessment, it's yours to keep either way."

## The Warm Approach (through a contact)

"[Mutual friend] mentioned you've been [struggling with X / looking to
improve Y]. I do exactly that — I spend an hour watching how your business
runs and come back with practical changes. Want to grab a coffee and I'll
tell you about it?"

## Follow-Up (after the assessment)

"Hey [Name], just wanted to check — did you get a chance to look at the
report? Any questions? ... If you'd like ongoing help implementing the
changes, I offer a monthly retainer for $149 that includes [specific
support based on their findings]."

## Key Messages

1. **Practical, not theoretical.** We find things you can change THIS WEEK.
2. **In-person observation.** We see things you've gone blind to.
3. **Affordable.** $199 is not a big bet. Most find it pays for itself fast.
4. **No lock-in.** The assessment is a standalone product. Retainer is optional.
5. **AI-assisted, human-delivered.** We're transparent about how we work.

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "I don't need help" | "That's great — most successful businesses don't think they do. But I've never done one where we didn't find at least one thing worth $200." |
| "I can't afford it" | "Fair enough. Here's my card — when the time's right, give me a call." (Don't push. Walk away.) |
| "What makes you qualified?" | "I've spent 15 years in tech — databases, cloud, DevOps. I know what tools exist and how they actually work in practice, not just in marketing material." |
| "Is this an AI scam?" | "Nah. I physically come here and watch how you work. The AI helps me analyse data faster — like your website, reviews, that kind of thing. But the recommendations come from what I see in person." |
| "I'm too busy" | "That's actually why this works — I do the hard part. You just need an hour for the visit and 30 minutes for the walkthrough." |
