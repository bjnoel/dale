# Business Plan — v2.0

## Mission
Build an ethical, AI-human partnership that generates $100+/month in recurring
revenue through two complementary tracks, with full public transparency.

## Strategy: Two Tracks

### Track A — Perth AI Efficiency Audits (Revenue)

**The pitch:** "I'll spend an hour in your business, and come back with 3-5
specific things you can change this week to save time or make more money.
No jargon, no 40-page report. Just practical stuff that works."

**Why it works in Perth:**
- Perth businesses are generally a few years behind on tech adoption
- Most don't realise how disruptive AI is about to be for their operations
- In-person trust matters more in Perth than in Sydney/Melbourne
- SEO audits start at $1,500+ from agencies — we're 10x cheaper and more practical
- We're not selling "AI consulting" (buzzword). We're selling results.

**The process:**
1. Benedict visits the business (1 hour observation + conversation)
2. Benedict takes notes on pain points, tools in use, manual processes
3. Dale analyses: website, online presence, operational inefficiencies
4. Dale produces deliverable: 3-5 specific, actionable recommendations
5. Benedict delivers in person with a 30-min walkthrough
6. Optional: ongoing retainer for implementation support

**Deliverable format:** TBD — needs to be professional but not overwhelming.
Probably a branded PDF with clear sections: What We Found, What To Change,
Expected Impact, How To Do It.

**Target verticals:**
- Retail: inventory management, online presence, POS integration, customer retention
- Professional services: booking systems, client communication, document automation
- Trades: quoting process, job management, invoicing, online presence

**Market research findings:**
- Perth SEO audits: $1,500-5,000 for one-off, $1,400-3,500/mo ongoing
- Our price point ($149-299) is positioned as accessible entry point
- Risk: perceived as "too cheap" by some. May need to adjust upward.

### Track B — Rare Fruit Stock Tracker (Moat)

**The opportunity:** Australia's rare fruit collectors rely on manually checking
individual nursery websites and Facebook groups to find sought-after varieties.
Stock of popular items (jaboticaba, rollinia, sapodilla) sells out within hours.
Nobody is aggregating this data.

**Known nurseries to monitor:**
1. Daleys Fruit Tree Nursery (Kyogle, NSW) — largest, most comprehensive
2. Ross Creek Tropicals (Gympie, QLD) — strong tropical selection
3. Heritage Fruit Trees — heirloom specialist
4. Exotica Rare Fruits Nursery — Asian/tropical focus
5. Heaven on Earth Fruit Trees — rare tropical specialist
6. Ladybird Nursery (Kallangur, QLD) — general + native
7. [More TBD — Benedict to identify WA-specific nurseries]

**Data to collect:**
- Plant name (common + botanical)
- Price
- Stock status (in stock / out of stock / pre-order)
- Nursery
- Date observed
- Shipping availability (esp. to WA — quarantine matters)

**Monetisation (future):**
- Free tier: basic alerts when specific varieties come in stock
- Paid tier: price history, trend graphs, seasonal patterns, price alerts
- Premium: scion/grafting wood availability (this is the real collector market)
- Possible: affiliate/referral fees from nurseries we drive traffic to

**Competitive landscape:** Nobody. This is greenfield.

## Go-to-Market

**Track A:**
1. Do 1-2 free audits for friendly businesses (portfolio pieces)
2. Set up landing page with case studies
3. Benedict approaches businesses in person (Scarborough, Leederville, Subiaco?)
4. Blog content: "5 things Perth retailers are getting wrong" type posts
5. The "AI business" narrative gets us press/attention on HN, Reddit, etc.

**Track B:**
1. Build monitoring silently
2. Share useful data in rare fruit Facebook groups / forums
3. Build email list of interested collectors
4. Launch paid tier once we have 3+ months of data and 100+ email subscribers

## Key Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Can't find Track A clients | Medium | High | Free first audits, Benedict's network |
| Track B market too small to monetise | Medium | Low | It's the moat track, not revenue track |
| Nursery blocks our scraping | Low | Medium | Respect robots.txt, offer value back |
| Benedict runs out of time | Medium | High | Build efficient async workflow |
| Competition copies us | Low (Track A), Very Low (Track B) | Medium | In-person moat, data moat |
