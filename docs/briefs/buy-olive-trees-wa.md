It is a TEMPLATE page. The same builder produces roughly 188 `buy-[species]-trees-[state].html`
combo pages (WA gets every species with 3+ in-stock items, QLD/NSW/VIC get the top 20 each).
The olive WA page is one instance of that template.

The page has three parts:
1. A live in-stock table (variety, nursery, price). This is the page's core job and it works.
   Keep it, and keep it high on the page.
2. A short amber "climate note" box near the top.
3. A long "Growing Olive in Western Australia" block at the bottom. This is the wall of text.

What is actually wrong (all verified by reading the builder and the live page):

- **The growing text is generic, not WA-specific, and uncited.** The "Growing Olive in Western
  Australia" block is just `species_info["description"]` (3275 chars) pulled from the olive entry
  in `fruit_species.json` (`build_species_state_pages.py` lines 242, 312-314, 358-359). The same
  text appears verbatim on the olive QLD, NSW and VIC pages and on `/species/olive.html`. Its
  heading promises Western Australia, but the body mentions South Australia and Victoria and says
  nothing specific to WA conditions, WA growing regions, WA pests, or WA harvest timing. It is one
  unbroken wall of text with no subheadings and no sources.
- **The climate note is miscategorised for olives.** `get_climate_note()` looks up
  `SPECIES_CLIMATE_CATEGORY["olive"]`, which is set to `"temperate"` (line 93), then renders
  `STATE_CLIMATE_NOTES["WA"]["temperate"]` (line 50): "South-west WA's mild winters suit temperate
  stone fruit and pome fruit ... Chilling hours may be lower, choose low-chill varieties." Olives
  are neither stone nor pome fruit and have effectively no winter-chill requirement, so the
  chill-hours advice is irrelevant and misleading on an olive page. (Grape is also tagged
  "temperate" and inherits the same wrong note, so the category model is too coarse.)
- **Copy-rule violations (no em or en dashes, per CLAUDE.md).** The price range is built with an
  EN dash: `f"${lo:.0f}–${hi:.0f}"` (line 257), so the live page shows "$25-$330" with a
  U+2013. The temperate climate note (line 50) contains an EM dash. Both are template-wide and
  affect every combo page, not just olive.
- **No citations, no FAQ, no structured data, no conversion path.** These high-traffic entrance
  pages have no sources, no FAQPage JSON-LD, and no alerts/Treesmith call to action.

So this is a "rebuild the editorial layer of a live template", NOT a re-home and NOT a 404 fix.
The plumbing is fine; the content is thin, generic, partly wrong, and breaks the copy rules.

## Substance to cover for olive in WA (verify all of it, do not assume)

- **Climate fit.** WA's south-west has a Mediterranean climate (hot dry summers, mild wet winters)
  that is close to ideal for olives. Olives need heat to ripen and do NOT need winter chill, so
  drop the chill-hours framing entirely. Note the warm dry inland and northern WA caveats
  (irrigation, very high heat).
- **WA growing regions.** Where olives are actually grown in WA (for example the Swan Valley,
  Perth Hills, Gingin/Moora, the Great Southern, Margaret River). Verify against WA sources.
- **Choosing varieties for WA.** Oil versus table versus dual-purpose, and which perform well in
  WA conditions. The page already lists Kalamata, Koroneiki, Arbequina, Manzanillo, Frantoio,
  Miniolea and others from live stock, so tie recommendations to varieties buyers can actually get.
  Cover self-fertility and the value of a pollinator (for example Pendolino).
- **Planting and soil.** Full sun, free-draining soil, sensitivity to waterlogging, and the WA
  reality of sandy soils (wetting agents, feeding, mulch). Container-grown olives can go in most of
  the year with summer water; cross-link to `/when-to-plant.html` for timing.
- **Water and feeding.** Drought tolerant once established, but young trees and fruiting trees need
  water through the dry WA summer. Realistic establishment watering.
- **Pests and diseases in WA.** Olive lace bug, anthracnose, peacock spot, black scale, and which
  pressures are higher or lower in WA. Mention WA quarantine context where relevant (it is why some
  eastern nurseries cannot ship here).
- **Harvest and curing.** WA harvest window (roughly autumn into early winter), green versus black,
  and that raw olives must be cured before eating.
- **Buying tips.** Grafted/known-variety stock over unnamed seedlings, typical pot sizes and prices,
  and a nudge to the live stock table above.
