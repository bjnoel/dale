# 2026-06-11 — Variety blurbs: plum, pear, quince, medlar

Added 16 verified "what's unique" blurbs to treestock `/variety` pages across four pome and stone
fruit species, continuing the DEC-178 rollout.

- **plum:** Spring Satin (the first plumcot from a public breeding program).
- **pear:** Shinseiki, Hosui and Ya Li nashi, plus the European pears Clapp's Favourite and the
  red-skinned Williams Red sport.
- **quince:** Smyrna, Champion, Pineapple, Missouri Mammoth, Rea's Mammoth, Chinese quince
  (Pseudocydonia, not a true quince), De Bourgeaut and Powell's Prize. First quince variety file.
- **medlar:** Dutch and Nottingham, the two classic heirloom medlars. First medlar variety file.

Every blurb is backed by at least two reputable sources with at least one non-nursery source
(RHS, USDA ARS, NC State Extension, Wikipedia, the American Horticultural Manual, and rare-fruit
societies). Thirty further variety strings were skipped: multi-graft combo listings, misspelling
duplicates of varieties already covered, generic descriptors, and a handful of cultivars too
obscure to verify (nursery-only sourcing). Skipping is the correct outcome here, never a guess.
