### DAL-208: Lychee meta CTR fix (treestock.com.au)

Found a high-impact SEO opportunity via Google Search Console query analysis.

**The problem:** The lychee species page was getting 1,682 impressions/month at position 9.8 (essentially page 1 of Google) but only 4 clicks — a CTR of 0.24%. For a page-1 result, you'd expect 3-5% CTR, which would mean 50-80 clicks.

**Root cause:** People searching specifically for Salathiel lychee trees ("salathiel lychee in stock", "salathiel litchi buy") were seeing our page at positions 8-10, but clicking past it because the title and meta description didn't mention Salathiel at all. They saw "Compare Prices | treestock.com.au" and assumed it wasn't what they wanted.

**The fix:** Added a `sought_varieties` field to the lychee growing guide JSON: `["Salathiel", "Wai Chee", "Kwai Mai Pink"]`. When any of those are in stock, the meta description now leads with "Track Salathiel, Wai Chee and more." — directly matching what searchers are looking for.

Live today: "Track Salathiel, Wai Chee and more. 43 Lychee varieties in stock across 8 Australian nurseries."

Salathiel is currently in stock at Daleys, Ross Creek Tropicals, Fruit Tree Cottage and Fruitopia — so the claim is truthful and the page delivers on it.

The mechanism is general: any species guide JSON can get a `sought_varieties` list when GSC shows variety-name queries with high impressions and low CTR.

## Trends page: maintained + de-corrupted (Benedict feedback, DEC-218)

Promoting the market-trends page into the new Insights menu surfaced two problems, both now fixed:

- **It was not being rebuilt.** The trends page is meant to run daily but had never been wired into the nightly pipeline, so it only refreshed on rare manual rebuilds and was not in the sitemap. It now rebuilds every night and is listed in the sitemap for search engines.
- **Every species' trend looked the same.** Benedict spotted that all the 30-day sparklines showed the same crash-and-recover zigzag. ## Rootstock and grafting reference layer (DAL-209, DEC-219)

Built a new guide, /rootstock.html, on the part of a fruit tree that grafters and serious collectors actually get to choose: the rootstock, the separate root system a named variety is grafted onto. It sets how big the tree grows, how soon it fruits, and how it copes with your soil and pests. Good Australian guidance on it is genuinely hard to find, so it is a strong long-tail search opportunity and it deepens the grafter audience that is treestock's core.

Benedict approved the ticket and made the three open calls: one hub page (not sections bolted onto species pages yet), a pilot of peach, plum and apple (apple added for its search volume, the M and MM dwarfing rootstocks), and dropping the bonus "heritage plums you cannot buy in WA" page.

The page covers, for each of the three:

- A rootstock comparison table: vigour and tree size, soil suitability, disease and pest resistance, and for apple the dwarfing percentages (from a super-dwarf courtyard tree on M27 to a full-size orchard tree on a seedling).
- How to grow your own: peach comes fairly true from a stratified stone, plum strikes readily from winter hardwood cuttings, and apple is the exception, because it will not come true from seed and the dwarfing stocks are clones propagated in stool beds, so grafters buy them.

Plus a cross-species section on where to buy rootstock in Australia (two retail nurseries, the rest wholesale) and, framed as restrictions rather than badges, what it takes to move rootstock into the quarantine states (WA, NT and Tasmania). Every fact on the page is cited to a real source: state agriculture departments, university extension, the RHS, peer-reviewed reviews, and Australian nursery rootstock pages.

It is a static layer by design. Rootstock is sold wholesale, seasonal and bare-root by phone and order form, so there is nothing to price-track day to day, which is exactly why a maintained reference page is the right format.

Built to mirror the existing growing-guides architecture: one JSON file per species plus a simple renderer, so the next species is one file, no new code. Linked from the Guides menu, added to the sitemap, cross-linked with the pollination and bare-root guides. Full test suite 1,762 green; deployed and verified live.

## History page: same de-corruption fix (Benedict follow-up, DEC-220)

Benedict asked to apply the trends fix to the history page too. It had the same root cause (the July 4 corrupted days) but a different symptom: instead of a phantom crash, the history page went quiet, hiding real activity. Because it compares each day to the day before and a corrupted day has almost no data, the day after each corrupted day lost its real change list. On live data, July 4 showed 0 changes when there were really 127, July 1 showed 10 when there were 192, and so on across about ten recent days.

Fixed by reusing the same coverage guard (now shared in one place so trends and history cannot drift), refined to compare each day against its neighbours rather than a global average (so the site's legitimately smaller early history is not dropped), and by comparing each day to the previous good day so the change list spans the gap. Verified against live data before shipping.
