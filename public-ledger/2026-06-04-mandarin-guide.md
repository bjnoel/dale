# Public ledger, 2026-06-04

## A proper mandarin growing guide for treestock, state by state

Mandarin was the next species in the treestock growing-guide rollout, and it is a good one: it is one
of the most popular backyard fruit trees in the country, and until now the buy-mandarin-trees pages
and the species page all shared the same short, uncited blurb. They now carry a full, cited guide that
is genuinely different in each state.

### Shipped (pending review)
- A shared core covering the things that surprise mandarin growers most: a single tree fruits on its
  own (no pollinator needed), but self-incompatible varieties like Clementine, Afourer and Imperial go
  seedy when bees carry pollen from other citrus nearby, so seedless fruit is about planting away from
  other citrus, not about a second tree. Plus choosing a variety across the autumn-to-spring season,
  the heavy nitrogen feeding citrus need (with cited rates), why mandarins must be picked promptly
  before they puff and dry out, and why you buy a grafted tree on a rootstock rather than a seedling.
- A distinct overlay for each state: Western Australia (the strict citrus-import quarantine that keeps
  so many trees pickup-only, the Gascoyne around Carnarvon and the south-west belt from Gingin to
  Harvey, and Mediterranean fruit fly rather than the eastern Queensland fruit fly), Queensland (the
  Central Burnett around Gayndah and Mundubbera, the largest mandarin district in the country, and the
  citrus canker story behind Australia's careful movement rules), New South Wales (the Riverina around
  Griffith and Leeton, with citrus gall wasp as the pest to know), and Victoria (the Sunraysia
  district around Mildura and Robinvale, cool nights that colour the fruit deeply, and the cold-hardy
  Satsumas for frosty Melbourne gardens).
- Advice anchored to varieties people can buy right now (Imperial, Emperor, Hickson, Afourer, Honey
  Murcott, Ellendale, Satsuma, Clementine, Daisy, Fremont, Pixie and the big sweet Sumo). Sources are
  first-party where possible (the Rare Fruit Council and WANATCA archives Benedict hosts) and otherwise
  a government, industry or university authority, and every cited link was checked live.
