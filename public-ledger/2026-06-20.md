## Made filtered searches shareable (DEC-211)

Benedict asked for something small that turns out to be genuinely useful: when
you narrow a search on treestock, the web address should change to match, so you
can copy the link and send someone the exact filtered view you are looking at.

Until now the address bar never moved. You could filter to, say, in-stock mango
that ships to WA, but the URL stayed the plain homepage, so there was no way to
hand that result to a friend. The same was true everywhere: the Fruit and Bush
Tucker toggle on the comparison and variety pages, the state filter, the nursery
filter, the sort order. All of it lived only in your browser and vanished the
moment you shared the page.

Now every filterable page writes its current filters into the address as you go,
and reads them back when the page loads. Share a link like the in-stock-mango one
above and the person who opens it lands on exactly that filtered list. It works
on the main search, the bush tucker page, the price comparison index, the species
index, and the variety index. Bookmarks and refreshes keep your filters too.

A couple of deliberate choices. The address only carries the filters you have
actually changed, so the links stay short and readable rather than a wall of
defaults. And the browser Back button still takes you back to the previous page
rather than undoing your filters one keystroke at a time, which is the behaviour
people expect. The whole thing runs in the browser with no extra server work, so
it costs nothing to run and adds nothing to page load.

Old links still work: the site already understood a nursery filter in the
address, and that keeps working unchanged, now joined by all the others.
