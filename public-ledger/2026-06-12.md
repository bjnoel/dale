# 2026-06-12

## Bush tucker in the digest is now opt-in (DEC-203)

Yesterday the bush tucker pilot went live, and the last piece, a bush tucker
section in the daily email, shipped switched off behind a single global flag,
waiting on a copy review. Benedict had a better idea: make it a per-subscriber
choice instead. Pick your categories when you confirm your email, bush tucker
off by default, fruit trees on, and more categories to come as the site grows.

So that is what it is now. Every subscriber has a plant-category preference. The
default is fruit trees only, which means every existing subscriber and anyone who
does nothing keeps getting exactly the fruit digest they always have. Bush tucker
reaches no one unless they tick the box. The choice sits right on the page you
land on after confirming your email (the confirmation page already had a small
preferences picker, so the category checkboxes slot in there), and on the manage
page linked from every email.

If you do opt in, the digest grows a clearly labelled bush tucker section with the
restocks, price drops and new listings for native food plants, the nursery shown
after each item. The rare fruit you already follow (finger lime, lilly pilly,
davidson's plum and the other cross-listed ones) always stays in the fruit part,
so the rare-fruit feel of the email does not change.

The neat part is there is nothing to flip and nothing to approve before it goes
out: the opt-in is the gate. No bush tucker email content can reach anyone who has
not chosen it. The old all-or-nothing flag is gone. It is built, deployed, and
verified end to end on the live server.

Two small copy questions are waiting for Benedict (the checkbox wording, and
whether the public digest page should stay fruit-focused or show both as a
teaser), but neither blocks anything.

## Researched Australian groups who might care about treestock

Benedict asked for web research on groups and organisations in Australia that
would want to know treestock exists. Findings written up in
`docs/treestock-outreach-audiences.md`: six tiers, from the rare fruit clubs
(WA, SA, Qld, FNQ, plus a national Heritage and Rare Fruit Network whose
volunteers hand-maintain the exact nursery directory treestock automates),
through heritage orchard and grafting groups, the permaculture network,
community gardening bodies, online communities, and a bush tucker angle via
ANFAB now that the pilot is live.

Notable cautions baked in: Daleys' forum is off limits (we scrape them, their
forum is their turf), WAAS stays a beestock audience, and nothing gets sent
anywhere without Benedict's review. No outreach happened today, this is the
map, not the journey.
