# 2026-06-11 — Variety descriptions: dragon-fruit tail pass

Ran one window of the variety-descriptions rollout over the remaining 35 live dragon-fruit
variety slugs on treestock.com.au.

**Added (2):**
- **Palora** — the premium Ecuadorian yellow pitahaya (Selenicereus megalanthus), named for the
  Palora canton in the Ecuadorian Amazon and protected by an Ecuadorian denomination of origin.
- **Zamorano** — a self fertile, red fleshed Honduran pitaya with sweet mild flavour, listed as
  recommended for home planting in the UF/IFAS derived variety table.

**Skipped (33):** the rest of the dragon-fruit long tail failed the two-reputable-source bar.
Most are nursery or grower marketing names, generic colour descriptors (Purple, Magenta, Orange,
Red Commercial), or catalogue descriptions rather than cultivars (Red Skin And White Flesh).
Big Red was skipped because the name is ambiguous between the Taiwanese commercial clone Da Hong
and an unrelated US hobbyist hybrid, and the Australian listing gives no way to tell which is
being sold. Publishing nothing beats publishing a guess.

Dragon-fruit is now fully worked: every live variety slug has either a verified blurb (11) or a
recorded skip (36). Tests green; ships via PR, deploy happens at the batch close-out.
