# 2026-06-11

## Beestock: beewise prices were missing GST (DEC-192)

Benedict spotted that a beewise listing (Konigin 25L stainless honey tank) showed
$232.73 on beestock while beewise sells it for $256. The ratio gave it away:
256 / 1.1 = 232.73, so we were publishing prices excluding GST.

**Root cause:** beewise is our one Magento retailer, and the Magento scraper read
the price from the dataLayer `productPage` event, which beewise emits ex-GST. The
same page's JSON-LD Product offer carries the inc-GST price the customer actually
pays. Every beewise price on beestock has been ~9% low since the retailer launched.

**Fix:** the scraper now prefers the JSON-LD offer price and only falls back to the
dataLayer event. Regression tests added (verified failing on the old code). Deployed,
beewise re-scraped, and the bee pages rebuilt the same day.

**No alert noise:** the correction shows up as a one-day ~10% price rise across
beewise, and the digest/alert engine only reports price drops, so subscribers get
no false alerts. Historical snapshots keep their old values; per-product GST status
can't be recovered retroactively, and a documented one-day step beats rewritten
history.

Shopify bee retailers and all treestock nursery scrapers use different code paths
and were never affected.

## Beewise also blocked our server three weeks ago (DEC-194)

Fixing the GST bug surfaced a second problem: beewise's firewall has rejected every
request from our scraper VPS since May 24 (a datacenter-IP block, it 403s browser
user-agents from that IP too, while our openly-identified bot is served fine from a
residential connection). The nightly pipeline logged the failure but marked it
non-fatal, so beestock quietly served three-week-old beewise prices on top of the
GST error.

Cleanup done today:
- One-time polite re-scrape (1 request/second, transparent bot UA) from Benedict's
  machine, so the site now shows current inc-GST beewise prices.
- The scraper now refuses to save a snapshot when more than 20% of page fetches
  fail, so a mid-run rate limit can never publish a half-empty catalogue.
- Found and fixed a data bug from beewise's launch: `latest.json` was a symlink to
  the April 19 dated snapshot, so every scrape since then silently rewrote that
  file. April 19 beewise data is unrecoverable; every other day is genuine.

We will not route around the block with proxies. Benedict will decide (Q43) whether
to ask beewise, a Perth business we send tagged referral traffic to, to allowlist
the monitor IP, or to drop the retailer instead.
