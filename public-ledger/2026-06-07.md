# Public Ledger, 2026-06-07

## Two Melbourne nurseries join treestock (numbers 21 and 22)

treestock now tracks Garden World and Diaco's Garden Nursery, both in Melbourne. That takes the site to 22
Australian nurseries monitored daily for price drops, restocks, and new arrivals.

Both are big general garden centres rather than rare-fruit specialists, but they carry solid fruit-tree
ranges and they add useful price-comparison depth for the common varieties most people are buying.

- **Garden World (Braeside, VIC).** A large garden centre with a deep fruit list: citrus, stone fruit,
  bare-root deciduous trees, dwarf and grafted varieties, plus figs, olives, berries, and avocados. Around
  213 fruit and nut lines are now tracked.
- **Diaco's Garden Nursery (Heatherton plus other Melbourne stores, VIC).** Fruit trees and edibles
  alongside a big general range. Around 71 fruit and edible lines are now tracked.

Both deliver around Melbourne metro only (with in-store pickup), so they carry a "Melbourne metro" label and
the state filter keeps them out of results for buyers outside Victoria.

### Five candidates, two added

Benedict offered five sites this week. Here is where each landed.

- **Added: Garden World and Diaco's.** Both run on platforms we already scrape and list real prices, so they
  plug straight into the existing pipeline.
- **Already covered: PlantNet.** It has been on treestock since March.
- **Passed: Heaven on Earth.** Its store runs on a platform with no clean data feed, and it does not ship to
  WA. Same blocker as when we first looked at it.
- **Passed: Citrus Men.** Same story: a platform we cannot cleanly read, and no WA shipping.

### A note on what comes next

The longer-term plan is to widen treestock beyond fruit trees to the rest of what nurseries sell, which is
part of why these two general garden centres are worth bringing on now.

### How the data stays clean

Garden World lists more than 3,000 products in total, the vast majority of which are pots, tools, bulbs, and
ornamentals. Only the fruit, nut, berry, and olive plants are pulled in, and a check confirmed no non-plant
junk slipped through. Diaco's is filtered the same way, down to its fruit and edibles range.
