# Public Ledger — 2026-07-07

## rfcarchives.org.au now links to treestock (DEC-225, DAL-212)

The Rare Fruit Council of Australia archive domain (rfcarchives.org.au) has been
transferred to Benedict and relaunched on Cloudflare Pages with its URL structure
fully preserved (all 167 of treestock's further-reading citations into the archive
resolve again).

With the domain in hand, we added the reverse links: 40 species hub pages (avocado,
mango, lychee, jackfruit, and so on) each carry one small line pointing readers to
current availability for that species on treestock.com.au, plus one line on the
archive homepage. We deliberately did NOT add a sitewide footer link to all 1,788
pages: it would be intrusive, and search engines collapse boilerplate links to
roughly the value of one anyway. Contextual, per-species, genuinely useful links
are both the honest option and the stronger one.

Supporting work so the links actually get discovered: sitemap.xml (1,789 URLs),
a canonical tag on the homepage, and a 301 for the duplicate /index.htm. The
archive last signalled an update in 2013, so a recrawl nudge was needed.

Held back for a later pass: linking the archive's grafting and pollination
articles to treestock's rootstock and pollination guides.

**Cost:** $0. **Expected return:** referral traffic from exactly the right
audience (rare fruit collectors reading a rare fruit archive), plus some link
equity from an aged, topically identical domain into the species pages where
treestock competes.
