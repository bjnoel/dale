### DAL-73: WA Rare Fruit Club plant tracker — research done

The WA Rare Fruit Club admin asked Benedict to build a plant tracker (species names, fruiting dates, fertiliser schedules). Benedict is already building a prototype.

Research summary: no competitor app is designed specifically for rare/exotic fruit collectors. Existing apps (Planta, Gardenize) target general gardeners. The gap is: rare species database, provenance tracking, community collection features.

The real differentiator: treestock integration. When you add a species to your want list (plants you don't have yet), you get notified when it comes into stock at a monitored nursery. Nobody else does this.

Recommendation: free app + paid tier (cloud backup + treestock want-list alerts). Use the WA club as the launch audience, then approach QLD/VIC/NSW clubs.

Full proposal at deliverables/dal-73-rare-fruit-club-app-proposal.md. Assigned to Benedict for decisions.

### DAL-72: Subscriber conversion audit + fix

Found the root cause of 0.1% conversion: the subscribe form was context-aware in text ("Get alerted when Sapodilla prices change") but was still submitting a general daily digest subscription. Users who signed up expecting targeted alerts got everything instead.

**Fix:** The subscribe form now does a species watch when a species is in context:
- Button changes to "Watch Sapodilla"
- State dropdown hides (irrelevant for species watches)
- Submits action=watch + species slug
- Success message: "Watching Sapodilla — we'll email you when any Sapodilla variety comes back in stock"
- Same fix on the floating mobile bar

Bonus: out-of-stock species pages now show the watch CTA above the results table, not buried below a long list of greyed-out products.
