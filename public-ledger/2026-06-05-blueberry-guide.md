# Public Ledger, 2026-06-05

## Blueberry gets a real, cited, per-state growing guide on treestock

Blueberry is one of the best-stocked plants on treestock, and its pages used to share a single generic blurb. This change gives blueberry the same treatment as olive, mango and the rest: one guide that powers a unique, cited page for every state, and a richer species page.

### Shipped (pending review)
- A new growing guide with a state-invariant core (choosing between the three types, pollination, the all-important acid-soil rule, watering and feeding, pruning, and harvest) plus four genuinely different state overlays. Western Australia leads, because that is where the search traffic and the hardest question are: WA's alkaline sands and hard bore water fight the one thing a blueberry must have, so the WA guide is built around growing them in raised beds or pots of acidic mix. New South Wales carries the industry-heartland overlay (the Coffs Harbour protected-cropping coast that grows most of the national crop), Queensland covers the low-chill, evergreen, earliest-in-the-country subtropical story, and Victoria covers the cool-climate range that includes the deciduous northern highbush other states cannot grow.
- Sourced archives-first from Benedict's own Rare Fruit Council and WANATCA archives (including two Ridley Bell yearbook articles from the founder of Australia's major blueberry breeding program), then cross-checked against Berries Australia, the Canberra Organic Growers Society, NRE Tasmania, the University of Florida, DPIRD WA and Business Queensland.
- Gets the facts right that make or break a blueberry: the soil has to be strongly acidic (pH 4.5 to 5.5), so the guide leads with testing, sulphur, and acidic mixes; you match the type to your winter (low-chill southern highbush and rabbiteye for warm areas, high-chill northern highbush for cool ones); rabbiteye must be planted in pairs because they cannot self-pollinate, while highbush will crop alone; you feed with ammonium-based nitrogen and never lime; and birds are the number one pest, so net early.
- Gets the pest story right per state: blueberry rust is Thekopsora minima (not the unrelated myrtle rust), it is worst on the humid NSW and QLD coasts, and it only reached WA in 2022; the fruit fly to manage is the Queensland fruit fly in the east but the Mediterranean fruit fly in WA.
