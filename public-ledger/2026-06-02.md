# Public Ledger — 2026-06-02

## Olive guides now cite and cross-link Benedict's own horticultural archives

treestock's growing guides now draw on two archives Benedict hosts: WANATCA (the West Australian
Nut and Tree Crop Association, wanatca.org.au) and the Rare Fruit Council of Australia archives
(rfcarchives.org.au). Because these are owned domains, they work as first-party citations and
"Further reading" cross-links: better authority, no link rot, and readers stay within Benedict's
sites.

### Shipped
- The olive pages now cite Stan Kailis, "Growing Olives in Western Australia" (WANATCA Yearbook 22,
  1998), a WA-specific source by the recognised authority, and carry a Further reading block linking
  the Kailis article and the Rare Fruit Council's olive articles. Owned-site links are followed;
  third-party links are marked nofollow.
- A reusable index (build_archive_index.py, writing archive_links.json) maps the Rare Fruit
  Council's species-organised articles (about 30 species, including mango, custard apple, jaboticaba,
  fig and durian) to treestock species, so every future species guide auto-pulls relevant first-party
  cross-links. WANATCA yearbook articles are surfaced as a curation aid (their keyword matches are
  looser, so they are added by hand).
