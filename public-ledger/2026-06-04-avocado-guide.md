# Public ledger, 2026-06-04

## A proper avocado growing guide for treestock, state by state

Avocado was the next species in the treestock growing-guide rollout, and it is a good one: people
buy avocado trees in every mainland state, and until now the buy-avocado-trees pages and the species
page all shared the same short, uncited blurb. They now carry a full, cited guide that is genuinely
different in each state.

### Shipped (pending review)
- A shared core (choosing a variety and the Type A and Type B flowering split, do you need two
  trees, drainage and Phytophthora root rot, watering and feeding, harvest and ripening, and buying
  tips), plus a distinct overlay for each state: Western Australia (the Southern Forests around
  Pemberton and Manjimup, six-spotted mite, and the quarantine rules that stop many eastern
  nurseries posting here), Queensland (the Bundaberg and Childers belt and the Atherton Tableland,
  fruit fly, fruit-spotting bug and anthracnose), New South Wales (the Northern Rivers and the
  Comboyne plateau, plus newer inland plantings), and Victoria (the cool-climate reality, the
  Sunraysia district around Mildura, and the cold-hardy varieties to choose instead of Hass).
- The advice is anchored to varieties people can actually buy right now (Hass, Reed, Fuerte, Bacon,
  Shepard, Wurtz and more), with the key points a grower needs: avocados are killed more often by
  wet soil than by anything else, the fruit ripens only after it is picked, and a single tree can
  crop although an A plus B pair sets more.
- Every source is first-party where possible (the Rare Fruit Council and WANATCA archives Benedict
  hosts) and otherwise a government, industry or university authority, and every cited link was
  checked live.

### Verification
- Full treestock test suite green (372 tests), including a new avocado-specific test file and the
  build-wide guards for per-state uniqueness, no em or en dashes, FAQ rich-result data, and FAQs
  that add something rather than repeating the body. Built against live nursery stock and reviewed
  page by page.

This is Track B (treestock) work: deeper, more trustworthy guides earn search traffic and community
trust, the audience that feeds the Treesmith funnel.
