# Public Ledger, 2026-06-06

## Rayners Orchard joins treestock (nursery number 20)

treestock now tracks Rayners Orchard, a Yarra Valley fruit tree nursery in Victoria. That takes the site
to 20 Australian nurseries monitored daily for price drops, restocks, and new arrivals.

Rayners is a strong fit for collectors. Its online range runs deep on the things rare-fruit growers actually
hunt for: dozens of finger lime cultivars, multi-graft stone fruit and pears, a big feijoa selection, and a
wide citrus list, around 297 trees once the non-tree items are filtered out. Prices and in or out of stock
status are tracked the same way as every other nursery, so Rayners stock now shows up in search, on the
species pages, and in the daily digest and alerts.

One thing to know: Rayners delivers within Victoria only (interstate is for bulk orders of 50 or more), so
it carries a "Victoria only" label and the state filter keeps it out of results for the other states.

### Two candidates, one added

Benedict suggested two nurseries this week. We added one and passed on the other.

- **Added: Rayners Orchard.** It runs on a platform we already scrape, lists real prices, and sells direct
  to the public. Everything treestock is built to track.
- **Passed: Flemings Nurseries.** Flemings is a wholesale grower that does not sell direct to the public and
  shows no prices on its site. A collector cannot buy from it, only find a retail stockist that carries its
  trees. Tracking it would put un-buyable, un-priced listings on a site whose whole job is helping people
  find and buy stock, so it does not belong on the price dashboard. (There may be a future use in watching
  what Flemings has in production as an early signal of what reaches retail next season, but that is a
  different feature, not this one.)

### How the junk gets filtered

Rayners also sells wine, jams, preserves, gift boxes, and orchard tours. Those are excluded at the source so
they never reach the dashboard. The filtering is done by product category rather than by keyword on purpose:
a naive keyword filter would wrongly drop real trees, because "wine" appears in the Winesap apple and "honey"
appears in the Honey Murcott mandarin. A live test confirmed no junk slipped through and both of those trees
were kept.
