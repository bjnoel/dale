# 2026-06-08 — Variety blurbs: olive, pomegranate, feijoa, lilly-pilly, jackfruit

Added 39 verified "what's unique" blurbs to treestock `/variety` pages across five species,
continuing the DEC-178 layer. Each blurb is one or two short paragraphs checked against two or
more reputable sources (at least one independent of any nursery), with a stored claims-to-sources
ledger and a confidence score. When a variety could not clear that bar it was skipped, not
guessed: 51 skips this run (thin-source cultivars plus duplicate spellings and non-cultivar
listing noise).

Per species (added / skipped / still remaining):
- Olive: 13 added, 18 skipped, 12 remaining. New: Arbequina, Frantoio, Picual, Arbosana,
  Coratina, Koroneiki, Leccino, Picholine, Barouni, Ascolana Tenera, Azapa, Mission, Correggiola.
- Pomegranate: 10 added, 10 skipped, 9 remaining. New: Ben Hur, Azerbaijani, Elche,
  Mollar de Elche, Kazake, Gayusha Rosavaya, Jodhpur Red, Angel Red, Eversweet, Parfianka.
- Feijoa: 9 added, 6 skipped, 2 remaining. New: Unique, Triumph, Mammoth, Apollo, Nazemetz
  (plus its Nazimet and Nazematze trade spellings), Kaiteri, White Goose.
- Lilly pilly: 5 added, 9 skipped, 29 remaining. New: Riberry, Brush Cherry, Resilience,
  Cherry Surprise, Bush Christmas.
- Jackfruit: 2 added, 8 skipped, 15 remaining. New: Black Gold, Honey Gold.

Sources leaned on the IOC World Catalogue of Olive Varieties, California Rare Fruit Growers,
Auckland Botanic Gardens, UF/IFAS, the WA rare fruit community's own RFCA archive, and
Wikipedia. Lilly pilly and jackfruit skipped heavily because most of their cultivars are nursery
trade names with no independent documentation. Tests pass (1401). Deploy is the batch close-out.
