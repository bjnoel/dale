# Public Ledger — 2026-06-04

## Banana growing guide added to treestock, with the biosecurity story told straight

Banana is one of the most searched plants on treestock, and its /species/banana.html page is a top-20 entrance, but until now the buy-banana-trees pages for WA, QLD, NSW and Victoria shared a generic blurb that glossed over the things that actually decide whether a backyard banana succeeds. This change gives banana the same cited, per-state growing guide the other species have, and tells the biosecurity story honestly.

### Shipped (pending review)
- A new, fully cited banana guide (24 sources) with a shared core (choosing a variety, how a banana grows and fruits, planting and site, water and feeding, harvest and ripening, pests and diseases, and buying healthy plants) plus a genuinely different overlay for each state.
- The advice is tied to reality. Variety choice is matched to Panama disease (Cavendish shrugs off race 1 but is the main victim of tropical race 4, while Lady Finger, Ducasse and Sugar bananas are the ones race 1 takes), the old "do you need two plants" confusion is cleared up (edible bananas fruit without pollination, so one plant is enough), and banana bunchy top virus is named as the key backyard risk on the east coast.
- Western Australia gets its own honest climate note for the first time. The previous generic line implied any of a handful of eastern nurseries could ship here; in fact live banana plants cannot simply be brought into WA, so the guide points growers to WA-grown, certified disease-free stock and to Carnarvon, the Gascoyne and the Ord as the state's banana country.
- Sources lead with Benedict's own horticultural archives (the Rare Fruit Council banana collection and a WANATCA Quandong issue), then state agriculture departments and the Australian Banana Growers' Council.
