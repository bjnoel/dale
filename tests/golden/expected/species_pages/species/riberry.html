Growing Riberry in Australia

Riberry (Syzygium luehmannii) is a coastal rainforest tree that produces heavy crops of small, pear-shaped, pinkish-red berries with a tart, clove-and-cinnamon flavour, popular for jams, sauces, and the bush food trade. It is one of the most ornamental and reliable bush tucker trees, with brilliant pink-red new growth and a naturally tidy form that also makes a good hedge or screen. It grows across subtropical and warm temperate coastal areas, preferring well-drained soil, full sun to part shade, and protection from heavy frost when young. Berries ripen from late spring into summer. Psyllid lerps can mark new growth as on other lilly pillies.
