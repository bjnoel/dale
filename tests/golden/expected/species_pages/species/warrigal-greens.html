Growing Warrigal Greens in Australia

Warrigal greens (Tetragonia tetragonioides) is a sprawling coastal groundcover used as a native leafy green, cooked like spinach. It was one of the first Australian plants taken into European cultivation. The fleshy, triangular leaves should be blanched briefly before eating to reduce naturally occurring oxalates. It is fast and easy to grow in full sun, tolerating sandy soils, salt spray, heat, and dry spells, and it self-seeds readily. Pick the soft growing tips regularly to keep the plant productive and tender. It works well as an edible groundcover or in a vegetable patch and grows across most of Australia.
